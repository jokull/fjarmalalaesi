# Safety Nets & Life-Event Finances — Reference (July 2026)

All amounts ISK, pre-tax unless noted. Values are 2026 and go stale —
verify at island.is / the named agency before relying.

**Recent law changes to know (2025–2026):**
- Dec 2025: fæðingarorlof caps no longer locked to the child's birth date —
  cap changes apply to all leave taken after they take effect
- Jan 1 2026: ALL residential leases must be registered (Leiguskrá HMS)
- Apr 1 2026: Menntasjóður reform (niðurfelling 20%+10%, repayment starts
  18 months after námslok, annuities to 40 years)
- Jul 1 2026: unemployment benefit period shortened 30 → 24 months (new
  applicants; lög 62/2026)
- Sep 1 2025: new disability system (lög 18/2024) — sjúkra- og
  endurhæfingargreiðslur + flat örorkulífeyrir + hlutaörorkulífeyrir

## 1. Fæðingarorlof (lög 144/2020)

- 12 months per child: 6+6, of which **6 weeks transferable** (max ~7.5
  months one parent). Expires at child's 24 months. Stretchable (50% leave
  + 50% work).
- **Payment: 80% of average heildarlaun** over a 12-month window **ending 6
  calendar months before the birth month** (self-employed: prior income
  year). Divisor never < 4 worked months; study/abroad months excludable.
- 2026: **cap 900,000/mo** (binds above 1,125,000 gross); floors ~276k
  (full-time), fæðingarstyrkur 262,061 (students) / 131,260 (outside labor
  market).
- Pension: 4% employee + **11.5% mótframlag paid by the fund**; but **no
  séreign employer match during leave** — high earners lose both surplus
  income (cap) and the 2% match.
- **Pitfalls to model**: (1) both parents simultaneously off burns 2×
  months at capped income; (2) cap-blindness — 1.6M gross → 900k = 56%
  replacement, not 80%; (3) the reference-window makes raises in the last
  ~15 months before birth partly invisible; (4) **umönnunarbil** — leave
  ends at 12 months, leikskóli starts ~18–24 months: budget dagforeldri
  months (see §5).

## 2. Atvinnuleysisbætur (VMST)

2026: grunnbætur **383,870/mo** (100% bótaréttur; scales with prior
starfshlutfall) + 15,355/child; **tekjutengdar 70% of prior wages, cap
605,159/mo, for 3 months** (after 10 working days at grunnbætur, once per
period). Frítekjumark 94,487/mo, then 50% reduction of excess.

- Bótaréttur accrues over the last 12 months (12 mo full-time = 100%; min
  3 mo = 25%). **Period now 24 months** for new applicants (Jul 2026).
- Quitting voluntarily / fired for cause / refusing work → **2-month
  biðtími** (repeat: 3 months; third: rights lapse).
- High-earner shape: 2 weeks ~192k → 3 months at 605k cap (binds above
  ~865k gross) → 384k thereafter.
- Ask VMST to deduct union dues from benefits — **maintains** sjúkrasjóður
  and fund rights while unemployed.

## 3. Illness & disability — the income ladder

Sequence and the cliffs (teach this as a ladder):

1. **Employer veikindaréttur** (~full pay): 2 days/worked month in year 1;
   2 months after 1 yr; 4 after 5 yrs; 6 after 10 yrs (+3 for work
   accidents).
2. **Union sjúkrasjóður**: ~**80% of wages ~6 months** (VR: 80% of 6-mo
   average, ~120–180 days; Efling: 180 days + extensions, long-members up
   to 100% capped ~1M/mo).
3. **The cliff**: SÍ sjúkradagpeningar ~**2,440 kr/DAY** (~73k/mo) — far
   below minimum wage; or, if in treatment/rehab, TR **sjúkra- og
   endurhæfingargreiðslur 416,950/mo** (up to 60+24 months).
4. **Örorkulífeyrir** (new system Sep 2025): flat **416,950/mo** (0–25%
   work capacity), hlutaörorkulífeyrir ~341,899 (26–50%) + virknistyrkur;
   heimilisuppbót +69,126. Frítekjumark (örorku): 105,200/mo over all
   income, 45% reduction above.
5. **Private insurance fills the gap**: a 1M/mo earner falls to ~42%
   replacement at step 3–4. Sjúkdómatrygging (critical illness) sized
   1–3× annual gross; líftrygging rule of thumb: short-term debts + ~2–3×
   annual salary + ~1M per child.

Self-employed have no step 1 and often thin step 2 — insurance matters
most for them.

## 4. Menntasjóður námsmanna (lög 60/2020, reformed Apr 2026)

- Borrowable 2026–27: grunnframfærsla **178,442/mo** + húsnæði **120,311**
  ≈ 299k/mo (single, renting; per úthlutunarreglur nr. 312/2026 — the
  school-year-2025–26 figures were never primary-verified, use these).
  Frítekjumark 2,600,000/yr, 45% reduction above.
- Interest-free until námslok; then choose verðtryggt (**cap 4%**) or
  óverðtryggt (**cap 9%**); rate = 3-yr avg of ~5-yr ríkisbréf yield +
  0.8%. Caps have been binding in recent years.
- **Niðurfelling: 20% granted per semester on progress + 10% on on-time
  completion** (= 30% total; dropouts keep earned portions). Changed from
  the single 30% at graduation.
- Repayment starts **18 months after námslok**; annuity 5–40 yrs, done
  before age 66; income-based option (fixed floor + 3.75% of annual
  income) only if námslok by age ~35.
- **Greiðslumat**: námslán don't block first-home buying, but the monthly
  payment counts as existing debt service — income-based repayment
  minimizes the hit.

## 5. Child benefits & childcare (Reykjavík)

- **Barnabætur 2026**: 345,000/child/yr (couples; 514,500 single parents)
  + 130,000 under-7 supplement. Phase-out ~4% per child above 11,688,000
  (couples) / 5,844,000 (single) — verify the exact schedule at
  skatturinn.is/barnabaetur before computing (post-2024 simplified system).
  Paid **4×/year** (Feb/May advance, Jun/Oct final), automatic, no
  application.
- **Meðlag 2026: 50,634/mo per child** (legal minimum, = TR barnalífeyrir).
  Collection now under Sýslumaðurinn á Norðurlandi vestra (Innheimtustofnun
  abolished 2024); TR pays recipients up front. Non-payment → wage
  withholding up to 50%, ahead of tax claims.
- **Leikskóli Reykjavík (from Apr 2026): first 36 h/week FREE** — only
  meals (~15,290/mo) at 36h; 42.5h max ≈ 53,290 all-in. Income and sibling
  discounts on the paid part.
- Frístundaheimili ~24,452/mo; school meals free (through 2027);
  frístundakort 75,000/yr (ages 6–18).
- **Dagforeldrar (the umönnunarbil)**: city subsidy 88,800/mo (couples) /
  121,608 (single) toward market-rate day-parents under 18 months — the
  parent-paid remainder is the biggest family cash-flow squeeze, made
  starker now that leikskóli is largely free.

## 6. Budgeting baselines — what greiðslumat actually uses

- Ministry neysluviðmið: **frozen at 2019 values — stale, don't use raw**.
- **UMS framfærsluviðmið** (updated every 2 months — the live reference,
  used in greiðsluaðlögun and de facto in greiðslumat), June 2026,
  EXCLUDING housing/utilities/insurance/childcare:
  single **238,101**; couple **335,290**; couple+2 **473,171**; each
  additional child ≈ +66–72k.
- Legal frame: consumer loans reglugerð 920/2013 (greiðslumat mandatory
  ≥2M); mortgages reglugerð 270/2017 — documented actual spending allowed
  but **never below 75% of neysluviðmið**.
- **Teaching point**: these are lean medians — a real Reykjavík family of
  four runs 900k–1.3M+/mo all-in. Passing greiðslumat ≠ affordable.

## 7. Debt trouble

- **Umboðsmaður skuldara (UMS)**: free state help — counseling,
  greiðsluaðlögun, and paying the 250,000 kr skiptatrygging for personal
  bankruptcy when broke.
- **Greiðsluaðlögun** (lög 101/2010): application triggers greiðsluskjól
  (collection freeze, no dráttarvextir); ~1–3 years living on UMS viðmið
  with pro-rata payments; unsecured debt forgiven at completion; mortgage
  within home value NOT written down (liens above value can be stripped).
- **Creditinfo vanskilaskrá**: listed at ≥50,000 kr, ≥40 days default +
  legal basis; **max 4 years; paid = removed immediately**. While listed:
  no credit score at all → no loans, cards, phone contracts; landlords and
  some employers screen it. **Removed entries drag the lánshæfismat up to
  4 further years.** Free self-check: Mitt Creditinfo (incl. who looked
  you up).
- **Personal bankruptcy 2-year rule** (lög 142/2010): claims expire 2 years
  from skiptalok — a genuine fresh start; sometimes faster than 3-year
  greiðsluaðlögun when there's no home to save.
- Consumer-loan cost cap: ÁHK ≤ 35% + policy rate (~43% mid-2026); the
  smálán loophole era is over. Dráttarvextir Jul 2026: **15.5%** (per SÍ
  notice 06/2026 — SÍ publishes monthly, don't compute from meginvextir).
  Collection-fee caps per reglugerð 37/2009 — %-of-debt fees prohibited.

## 8. Renting (húsaleigulög 36/1994 as amended 2024–2026)

- **All leases must be registered** (Leiguskrá HMS, 30 days) since Jan
  2026; island.is e-contracts auto-register.
- Rent fixed during terms ≤12 months (indexation only in ≥13-month fixed
  terms); adjustments toward markaðsleiga need ≥3 months' substantiated
  written notice; disputes → kærunefnd húsamála (free, binding).
- **Deposit max 3 months**; landlord has 4 weeks after move-out to repay
  or make an itemized written claim.
- Uppsagnarfrestur (indefinite): apartments **6 months**; landlords can
  only terminate on enumerated grounds since 2024.
- Benchmark rent with HMS **Leiguverðsjá** (near-full market coverage now
  that registration is universal).
- **Bjarg íbúðafélag** (ASÍ/BSRB nonprofit cost-rent): income caps
  ~9.1M/yr single, 13.5M couples +2.25M/child; requires union membership
  16 of last 24 months.
- **Búseturéttur** (Búseti): buy-in ~10% of unit value; resale = original
  price + CPI only — **no market appreciation**; monthly búsetugjald
  covers building costs. Cheaper entry and tenure security vs renting; no
  leverage on house prices vs owning.
