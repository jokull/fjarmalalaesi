# The Icelandic Pension System — Reference (July 2026)

The three pillars, how rights accrue, choosing providers, and the rules
young people actually ask about. All amounts ISK, 2026 values.

**Two facts that correct common priors:**
1. **Tilgreind séreign DOES reduce TR ellilífeyrir** (since Jan 2023) —
   only *frjáls* séreign is TR-immune. Sources conflating "séreign" hide
   this.
2. The statutory pillar-2 target is now **72%** of career-average wages
   (1.8%/yr × 40 yrs, lög 55/2022) — the often-quoted 56% is the old law.

## 1. The three pillars

**Pillar 1 — TR (almannatryggingar)**: full ellilífeyrir **365,592/mo**
(40 residence-years 16–67, proportional otherwise) + heimilisuppbót
92,384 if living alone. **Skerðing 45%** of income above frítekjumark
(43,658/mo general; 210,400/mo extra for employment income). A full
pillar-2 pension (~400k/mo) wipes out most TR. Early from 65 (−12.24%
permanently); deferral +0.5%/mo to max +30%.

**Pillar 2 — Skyldulífeyrir (samtrygging, lög 129/1997)**: mandatory
**15.5%** of wages (4% employee + 11.5% employer), ages 16–70, employed
AND self-employed (of reiknað endurgjald).

- **Age-priced accrual** (aldurstengd ávinnsla): each contribution buys
  lifelong CPI-indexed annual pension per the fund's réttindatafla —
  young money buys ~2× what 55-year-old money buys. Gildi's table:
  10,000 kr contributed at 25 → **1,684 kr/yr for life from 67**; at 40 →
  1,061; at 55 → 831. Teach this: *early contributions are the cheap ones*.
- Buys a hidden insurance package too: **örorkulífeyrir with
  framreikningur** (rights projected to retirement age if you've paid ~3
  of last 4 years — a large invisible disability policy), makalífeyrir
  (50% of rights to surviving spouse, time-limited), barnalífeyrir
  (fund-specific, ~28–57k/mo per child).
- Drawing flexible 60–80 with actuarial adjustment (Gildi: 65 → ×0.865,
  70 → ×1.264, 80 → ×3.52).
- **Rights are adjustable, not guaranteed** (39. gr.): >10% actuarial
  imbalance (or >5% for 5 yrs) forces symmetric cuts or increases. Real
  history: Gildi −12%/−8% (2009–10), cumulative ~−30% post-crash; LIVE
  +10%/+12% (2021/23).

**Pillar 3 — Frjáls séreignarsparnaður**: employee 2–4% + **2% employer
match conditional on employee ≥2%**. **The match is a 100% instant return
on the matched portion — free salary forfeited by not opting in.** EET
tax; free withdrawal from 60; **does not reduce TR** (the only savings
with this property); fully heritable; creditor-protected.

## 2. Tilgreind séreign — the 3.5% choice

Private-market workers (and LSR/Brú A-deild) may redirect up to **3.5pp
of the employer's 11.5%** into a personal account instead of samtrygging.
Default is all-in samtrygging; opt-in is written, forward-only,
reversible, and the provider is freely chosen.

| | Frjáls séreign | Tilgreind séreign | Samtrygging |
|---|---|---|---|
| Withdrawal | 60, free | **62, forced equal monthly spread until 67** | annuity 60–80 |
| Inheritance | full | full | none (only maka-/barnalífeyrir) |
| **TR clawback** | **none** | **YES since 2023** | yes |
| Insurance accrual | — | **forfeited on those iðgjöld** | full |
| Mortgage redirect | fyrsta íbúð ✓, general program ✓ | fyrsta íbúð ✓ (after frjáls), **general program ✗** | ✗ |

Framing: trades collective insurance + TR-immunity for ownership and
bequest. Best for high earners who'll be fully TR-skertir anyway; worst
for sole earners with dependents or elevated disability risk.

## 3. Choosing funds & providers

- **Samtrygging fund is usually locked** by kjarasamningur (VR → LIVE,
  Efling → Gildi, iðnaðarmenn → Birta, public → LSR/Brú). Free choice
  only outside kjarasamningar (self-employed, ehf owners → Almenni-
  Lífsverk, Frjálsi, Íslenski). The real free choices: séreign provider,
  tilgreind provider, ávöxtunarleið.
- **Compare samtryggingarsjóðir** on: tryggingafræðileg staða (2024/25:
  Stapi +4.4%, LSR A +1.2%, Gildi −1.2%, LIVE −5.4%, Birta ≈−6%),
  réttindabreytinga history, and hrein raunávöxtun (FME's annual
  samantekt; system-wide real: +2.6% 2025, ~4.0% 10-yr).
- **Séreign fees — the compounding killer**: fund séreignardeildir
  ~0.2–0.6%/yr; banks' products 0.55–1.4% (Kvika 0.55–0.80%, Almenni
  Ævisafn II 0.55%, Íslandsbanki ~1.03%, Arion erlend ~1.32%). At
  60k/mo over 40 years, 1.32% vs 0.2% fees ≈ **84M vs 113M outcome**.
  Fee differences compound more reliably than return differences persist.
- Ávöxtunarleiðir: prefer age-linked líftímaleiðir (auto de-risking) for
  default advice; innlánsleiðir for capital stability near withdrawal.
- Return comparisons: lifeyrismal.is "Ávöxtun sjóða" and
  aurbjorg.is/lifeyrismal/sereign — compare only *raunávöxtun within the
  same risk class*.

## 4. Lífeyrisgáttin

Rights aggregator (Landssamtök lífeyrissjóða; 18 funds). Access via your
own fund's Mínar síður (rafræn skilríki). Shows **áunnin** (accrued) and
**framreiknuð** (projected) samtrygging rights across every fund you've
paid into — the input for the retirement-income leg of any model.
Limitations: no séreign balances, no TR integration (model the 45%
skerðing separately), projections assume current salary continues.

## 5. Rules young people ask about

- **Moving abroad**: samtrygging rights never lost — CPI-indexed, paid
  worldwide from retirement age. Refunds only for citizens of
  non-agreement countries (EEA, UK, Canada, **USA since 2019** are all
  agreement countries — no refunds). Framreikningsréttur (disability
  projection) lapses ~1 year after contributions stop.
- **Self-employed**: must pay the whole 15.5% of reiknað endurgjald.
  Classic failure: invoice as verktaki, never remit — Skatturinn catches
  it at álagning, arrears to Söfnunarsjóður with surcharges, plus lost
  insurance and compounding.
- **Student gaps**: zero pillar-2 accrual (no voluntary buy-in exists);
  the hidden cost is the insurance lapse — though gaps ≤36 months for
  nám/leyfi/barneignir restore framreikningur 6 months after resuming.
- **Divorce**: samtrygging is personal by default — a big asymmetry for
  part-time/home spouses. Optional mutual-and-equal split of marriage-era
  ellilífeyrisréttindi (before drawing, by age 65). **Séreign IS divided**
  (hjúskapareign under helmingaskipti).
- **Death — the biggest literacy point**: samtrygging pays your estate
  **nothing** if you die single and childless (that's the price of the
  shared insurance); with survivors, time-limited makalífeyrir + per-child
  barnalífeyrir. **Séreign erfist að fullu** (maki 2/3, börn 1/3), taxed
  as income at payout but **no erfðafjárskattur** — another argument for
  capturing the match.
- **First home / mortgage redirect**: see `sereignarsparnadur.md` —
  fyrsta íbúð active (500k/yr, 10 yrs); the general redirect is permanent
  since June 2026 (apply before Dec 1 2026 for retroactivity).

## 6. Macro context

Assets **~9,463 ma.kr. (~186% of GDP)** end-2025 — world top tier.
Mercer 2025: **#2 of 52, grade A**, highest sustainability sub-score of
all systems. Rights are relatively secure because pre-funded
(sjóðsöfnun), not pay-as-you-go — but they are actuarial promises with a
statutory symmetric-adjustment mechanism, not state guarantees.

## Caveats

Barnalífeyrir/makalífeyrir vary ~2× between funds — always check the
specific fund's samþykktir. lifeyrismal.is still quotes the stale 56%
figure in places. TR deferral: reglugerð says max +30%, statute allows
actuarial deferral to 80 — unresolved tension. skatturinn.is pages lag
the June 2026 redirect law.
