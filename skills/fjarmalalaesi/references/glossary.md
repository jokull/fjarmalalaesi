# Glossary — Icelandic ⇄ English Financial Terms

Key terms for communicating accurately about Icelandic personal finance.
Icelandic-first, grouped by domain, with usage notes where terms are easily
confused. Use the Icelandic term (with English gloss on first mention) when
writing for Icelandic users; several concepts have no exact English
equivalent and translating them loses precision.

## Writing conventions

- **Amounts**: `1.234.567 kr.` — period as thousands separator, decimal
  comma (`4,49%`). Common abbreviations: `þús.` (thousand), `m.kr.`
  (million ISK), `ma.kr.` (billion ISK).
- **kennitala** — national ID (person or company), format `DDMMYY-XXXX`.
- **Dates**: `1. júlí 2026`. Months are not capitalized.
- **gjalddagi** vs **eindagi** — *gjalddagi* is the due date; *eindagi* is
  the final day to pay before penalty interest (dráttarvextir). A bill can
  have both; don't translate both as "due date".

## Phrasing tips for agents writing Icelandic

- Say **"verðtryggt lán ber raunvexti"** — indexed loans carry *real* rates;
  never present a verðtryggður vaxtaprósenta as comparable to óverðtryggðir
  nafnvextir without adding expected verðbólga.
- **"að greiða inn á höfuðstól"** = making an extra principal payment;
  **"að greiða upp lán"** = paying it off entirely.
- **"skerðing"** is the standard word for benefit clawbacks — "TR skerðir
  ellilífeyri um 45% af tekjum umfram frítekjumark".
- Use **"ráðstafa séreign inn á lán"** for the redirect program (the legal
  term is *ráðstöfun*), not "taka út séreign" (which means withdrawal —
  taxable, different thing).
- **launþegi vs verktaki** framing matters in any income discussion — always
  establish which one applies before computing take-home pay.
