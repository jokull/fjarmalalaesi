For the young-person question "where do I keep and grow money?". Rates

## 1. Institution map

- **Big three banks**: Arion (private, listed; pension funds are the big
  owners), Íslandsbanki (**fully private since May 2025** — state stake
  sold), Landsbankinn (~98% state-owned).
- **indó sparisjóður**: free debit account + Mastercard, savings pots
  ("sparibaukar"), small loans. No credit cards, no mortgages. FX markup
  **0.7%** since June 24 2026 (the famous 0% era ended) — still ~3× cheaper
  than the banks. Zero card/transaction fees.
- **Auður** (Kvika's brand): savings app + mortgages only; currently the
  highest unrestricted deposit rate.
- Sparisjóðir: three tiny regional survivors. Meniga: legacy PFM app.
- **Card FX abroad**: Arion 2.5/2.7%, Íslandsbanki 2.5%, Landsbankinn
  2.3%, indó 0.7%. Banks waive most basic fees under age 23–24.
- Practical young-person stack: indó for daily spending + travel; a bank
  relationship for credit card, mortgage, and verðbréfaþjónusta.

## 2. Deposit safety

- TIF renamed **TVF** (Tryggingarsjóður vegna fjármálafyrirtækja, tvf.is;
  lög 98/1999 as amended by 48/2022).
- Coverage: deposits incl. accrued interest up to **EUR 100,000
  equivalent (~14–15M kr) per depositor per institution**. Fund units and
  securities are NEVER covered (market risk is not insurable).
- **indó and Kvika (= Auður) are full members** — same protection as the
  big banks. Note: Auður + Kvika deposits share ONE cap (same institution).
- No hard statutory payout deadline (the EU 7-day directive still not
  implemented in Iceland). The 2008 "innstæður eru tryggðar að fullu"
  declaration was political, never law.

## 3. Savings rates & the real-return math (July 2026)

Óbundnir (unrestricted): Auður **7.25%**, Arion Fríðindareikningur
7.15–7.55% (tiered), Landsbankinn Markmið 7.15%, Íslandsbanki Ávöxtun
6.70%, indó sparibaukar 6.35%. 30-day-notice variants ~+0.4pp. Fixed
terms (3–12 mo): 7.4–7.85% — flat/inverted, market pricing cuts.
Verðtryggðir (3y+ binding): 1.8–2.7% real.

**The real-return math — the key literacy point:**
- Before tax at 5.2% inflation: best accounts ≈ **+2pp real**.
- After 22% fjármagnstekjuskattur: ≈ **0 real** (7.25% → 5.66% net).
- **The frítekjumark is the whole game**: 300,000 kr/person/yr of
  tax-free capital income (600k couples). At ~7.15%, that shelters about
  **4.2M kr per person** (8.5M/couple). Below that balance, real after-tax
  returns are solidly positive; above it, roughly zero. Plan account
  ownership (splitting between spouses) around it.

## 4. Government bonds for individuals

- No direct auction access (primary dealers only). Retail buys on the
  secondary market via bank verðbréfaþjónusta — at Landsbankinn/
  Íslandsbanki single bonds are **phone-only**, not in the apps. Funds are
  the app-friendly route.
- Series: **RIKB** (nominal), **RIKS** (verðtryggð), RIKV (T-bills).
  2026 auction yields: ~2.5–3yr nominal 6.9–7.7%; 12yr 6.5–6.8%; RIKS
  ~2.75–2.8% real → breakeven inflation ~4%.
- Costs: ~0.3–0.4% commission with 3,500–4,000 kr minimums + small annual
  custody — uneconomical under ~1M kr per trade.
- Tax: 22% as vaxtatekjur; **the 300k frítekjumark applies**. Quirk:
  verðbætur on RIKS are taxable interest — you pay tax on inflation
  compensation, so RIKS carry a heavier real-tax drag than RIKB at equal
  breakeven.

## 5. Funds, markets, abroad, crypto

- **Fee rules of thumb** (umsýsluþóknun/yr): money-market 0.35%; govt-bond
  funds 0.7–1.0%; blandaðir 0.5–1.5%; active Icelandic equity 1.45–1.85%.
- **Cheap index exposure exists**: IS Úrvalsvísitölusjóðurinn **0.40%/yr**
  (OMXI15 CAP, min 10,000 kr) and Landsbréf **LEQ** UCITS ETF (OMXI10, the
  only Icelandic ETF). Don't let anyone claim Iceland has no index funds.
- Kauphöllin (Nasdaq Iceland): 27 main-market companies; buy in bank apps;
  ~0.75% commission (Arion no minimum; ÍSB min 2,000 kr).
- **Investing abroad is unrestricted** (capital controls gone since 2017).
  Interactive Brokers and DEGIRO accept Icelandic residents; fund in
  EUR/USD (pay the bank's FX spread). **Foreign brokers don't report to
  Skatturinn** — you file RSK 3.19 (shares) / section 3.2 (accounts);
  treaty relief on foreign dividend withholding capped at treaty rate.
- **Crypto**: legal, MiCA in force (lög 101/2025); first licensed CASP
  June 2026 (Myntkaup). Tax: 22% on realized gains; **crypto-to-crypto
  swaps and spending are taxable disposals**; no frítekjumark; losses
  offset same-coin-same-year only; lost keys not deductible.

## 6. Investment taxation — the matrix

Rate **22%** on capital income. Key mechanics:

| Asset | Taxed when | Frítekjumark? | Loss offset |
|---|---|---|---|
| Deposit/bond interest | accrual/payment (withheld quarterly) | **yes** | — |
| Listed-share dividends | distribution (withheld) | **yes** (combined pot) | — |
| Listed-share gains | sale | **yes** (combined pot) | vs other share gains, same year only, no carry-forward |
| Fund units | **only at redemption** (tax-deferred!) — classified as vaxtatekjur | yes (interest leg) | — |
| Unlisted shares | sale | no | same-year share gains |
| Crypto | any disposal incl. swaps | **no** | same coin, same year |
| Rental (residential, ≤2 units) | álagning | 25% tax-free share (2026) → 16.5% effective | no expenses |

- The 300k frítekjumark is **one combined pot**: interest → dividends →
  listed-share gains, in that order. Banks withhold 22% regardless; the
  refund comes at álagning.
- From income year 2026, unused persónuafsláttur can **no longer** offset
  capital income (hits students/low earners).
- **Nominal-return taxation**: verðbætur, FX gains, and inflation-driven
  gains are all taxable — at 5.2% inflation the effective tax on the
  *real* return of an indexed deposit can exceed 60%.
- Nýsköpunar share deduction extended through 2026 (50–75% of investment,
  RSK-preapproved unlisted companies, 3-yr holding) — verify the current
  percentage before citing.

## 7. Expensive-credit traps & protection

- **Current unsecured rates**: overdraft ~15.5% at all three banks;
  credit-card revolving ~15.5%; dráttarvextir **15.5% per SÍ notice
  06/2026** — SÍ publishes the figure monthly (statutory formula adds a
  fixed vanefndaálag to a reference rate that is NOT simply meginvextir;
  cite the notice, don't compute it).
- **BNPL true costs**: Pei 60-day ≈ 4.95% + fees ≈ 40%+ annualized;
  Netgíró/Síminn Pay/Aur dreifing 17–18% + 3.5–6% upfront + ~400–550 kr
  per payment. Fee stacking pushes small balances to the legal ceiling.
- **Smálán cap**: ÁHK ≤ 35pp + policy rate (≈43% now). The offshore
  loophole era is dead (eCommerce 2020 bankrupt 2024); legal
  ceiling-priced lenders remain.
- **The single highest-value rule for young people**: paying off 15.5%
  debt beats saving at 7% — always kill overdraft/revolving/BNPL before
  building savings (the only exception: a minimal emergency buffer).
- Complaints: FME supervises but doesn't resolve disputes; the real route
  is **úrskurðarnefnd um viðskipti við fjármálafyrirtæki** (10,000 kr fee,
  refunded if you win). Neytendastofa enforces the consumer-credit law
  (fines, not compensation).
